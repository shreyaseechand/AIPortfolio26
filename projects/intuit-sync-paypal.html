Overview

Intuit's Small Business Group partnered with PayPal to let customers securely pull their sales transactions into QuickBooks Online through an automated plugin. By the time I joined, a Beta existed — and it was struggling.

Two problems dominated: a complicated PayPal-to-QBO account mapping that made people abandon the setup page, and lengthy, broken workflows that made transferring transactions cumbersome. I led design of the entire feature, focused on the customer pain while keeping two companies' teams aligned.

Research

Two very different users, one broken hand-off

Sitting in on product feedback calls, I found two distinct segments to serve at once:

  • Skilled accountants who know bookkeeping cold and want control.
  • Novice users with no bookkeeping experience who need guidance.

A heuristic evaluation against Nielsen's ten usability principles pinpointed the core failure: bouncing customers between two companies' systems — each with its own framework — created friction and abandonment.

Approach

The Laws of Simplicity, applied

The guiding principle was simple to say and hard to earn — a clear, intuitive, user-friendly setup. I leaned on the Laws of Simplicity, separating the two kinds of complexity:

01
Reduce actual complexity

Cut real steps and decisions out of the flow first.

02
Then reduce perceived complexity

Make what remains feel calm, ordered, and understandable.

03
Feature the common case

Put prominent, frequent use-cases front and center.

04
Rare tasks on demand

Keep infrequently used tasks available, but out of the way.

The redesign

From a mapping wall to a guided setup

Problem

Account mapping stopped people cold

The Beta asked customers to reconcile PayPal and QuickBooks accounts up front — a dense, high-stakes step that sent setup-page abandonment climbing.

Solution

A simpler, guided flow

I reworked setup into a clearer sequence, iterating through wireframes with content and engineering to keep tone and interaction patterns consistent — and specs pixel-perfect.

An educational blank slate. After setup, customers land on a sales-transactions page with a blank state that teaches the feature and prompts a first step — with entry points to related flows (PayPal expenses, bank transactions) surfaced only when they aren't yet configured.

Outcomes & reflection

An honest, mixed result

The redesign shipped, and the reception was genuinely mixed — a useful thing to sit with. Customers appreciated the improved setup and the granular control over which timeframe of transactions to import. The criticism that remained centered on accuracy and data-transfer reliability — largely product and integration issues downstream of design, and exactly the kind of trust problem that a good setup flow can't paper over on its own.
